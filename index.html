About Me

I am currently a Data Scientist at Nyansa where I conduct large scale analysis of IT network deployments. Looking at the full network stack we automatically baseline performance, detect anomalies and recommend remediations as well as systemic improvements. I design, prototype and implement in production those algorithms that analyze hundreds of GBs every day.

Previously, I obtained a Master's degree from Stanford University in applied mathematics with a strong focus on data science.

Prior to joining Stanford I worked on jet engines at Rolls-Royce. There, I built a set of modeling frameworks to improve the manufacturing process of gas turbine components. Using Computational Fluid Dynamics and Finite Element Analysis I was able to create highly customized models for a novel process called Metal Injection Molding.

I have a BSc in Operations Research from École des Ponts et Chaussées, France's oldest and one of the most reputable engineering schools. There, I also worked as a research intern at the applied mathematics lab where I created a decision model for cities facing flooding risk using Dynamic Programming.

My lifelong passion for flying has led me to get the French skydiving license. I also fly an RC plane in my free time.

Social Dynamics on StackOverflow

  • Date: December 2014


This project was realized in a team of three for the Computational Social Science class at Stanford.

Using data dumps from StackOverflow (>80GB) we have studied the influence of getting the Legendary badge on the behavior of hardcore users. Based on our results it appears that after getting this badge (the hardest to get) users tend to answer less frequently. This suggests that there is indeed an incentive to answer in order to obtain such badges.

We also conducted descriptive analyses of the demographics of the users of StackOverflow.

Job classification based on LinkedIn summaries

  • Date: June 2015


This project was realized in a team of two for the Deep Learning for NLP class at Stanford.

We used data provided by the company HiQ Labs that consisted of job title and job summaries as filled by users on LinkedIn. We reduced the number of output classes (job categories) using word vectors trained by Google and then matched these to job categories as defined in the ISCO-08 standard.

Once we had defined our output class for each job summary we trained a neural network to accurately predict the job category by reading the job summary as filled by the user.

This method reached an impressive accuracy of 31.7% to be compared with the baseline of 16% (predicting only the most common class).

Stochastic viability of cities facing flooding risk

  • Date: June 2013


This document has been created following my research internship at the applied mathematics lab of Ecole des Ponts.

During my internship I developed a model of a city facing flooding risk. Decision makers want to protect the city by improving the drainage system and the seawall but they are constrained by economic resources. They also want to minimize the costs induced by floods. My model finds the best course of action using historical data about the floods and assesses the risk that the city faces.

After my internship I was offered a Teaching Assistant position for which I created this practical class. The aim was to illustrate the use of stochastic control on a real-life example and walk the students through a step-by-step Scilab model.
